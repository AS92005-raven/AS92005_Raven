What is the effect?

Deforestation

Climate Change. Trees that are absorbing most of the CO₂. When these trees are chopped down, the CO₂ are going straight into the atmosphere instead of being absorbed by the trees, and it also damages the spiritual connection between the people and the land. Kaitiakitanga is passed down generation to generation so there is someone to take care for the environment. Clear-cutting forests leaves behind a damaged planet, leaving or role as protectors of the land.

Waste Management

If our waste are not managed properly, it can degrade ecosystems through soil contamination and contributing greenhouse gas into our atmosphere. Uncontrolled dumping and plastic in the ocean, river or sea can damage coral reefs kill many marine life through entanglement. When we allow this to happen, means that we diminish mauri. Practicing Kaitiakitanga means protecting the environment so future generation can continue the guardianship.

Industrial emissions

Emissions can change up temperatures and alter weather patterns that can impact our taonga such as kaimoana, forests and coastal fisheries, limiting the ability to sustain. Industrial emissions can damage our atmosphere, resulting in sickness in our world and can affect our mauri or life force. These emissions can release carbon dioxide, methane and other harmful gas into our atmosphere that affects the air and water quality.
